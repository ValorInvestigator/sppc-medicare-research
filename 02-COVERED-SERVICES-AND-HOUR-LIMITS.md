# Oregon SPPC — Covered Services and Hour Limits

> Source: OAR 411-034-0020 (APD/AAA track) and OAR 411-455-0050 (ODDS track)
> Last amended: APD 57-2022, effective December 23, 2022

---

## 1. Covered Personal Care Tasks (OAR 411-034-0020) — APD/AAA Track

### Core ADL Services

**1. Basic Personal Hygiene**
Providing or assisting with: bathing (tub, bed bath, shower), washing hair, grooming, shaving, nail care, skin care, foot care, oral hygiene.

**2. Toileting, Bowel, or Bladder Care**
Assisting to and from bathroom, on and off toilet, commode, bedpan, or urinal. Includes continence care, catheter care, and ostomy care.

**3. Mobility, Transfers, or Repositioning**
Assisting with ambulation or transfers with or without assistive devices. Includes bed mobility, wheelchair transfers, and repositioning for pressure sore prevention.

**4. Nutrition**
Preparing meals and special diets, assisting with adequate fluid intake or adequate nutrition. Includes feeding assistance, monitoring for choking/swallowing problems, and enteral feeding support where delegated.

**5. Medication or Oxygen Management**
Assisting with ordering, organizing, and administering oxygen or prescribed medications. Includes assisting with self-administration per prescription.

**6. Delegated Nursing Tasks**
Nursing tasks delegated by a licensed nurse in accordance with OAR 851-047 (Oregon State Board of Nursing delegation rules) and OAR 411-048. Examples: wound care, insulin injection, catheter irrigation — when properly delegated to a homecare worker by a supervising RN.

### Supportive / Incidental Services (IADLs)

When core personal care services are essential and authorized, the provider may also perform:

- Light housekeeping and laundry (for the individual's health and safety)
- Arranging medical appointments
- Observing the individual's health status and reporting significant changes to physicians or health care professionals
- First aid and handling of emergencies
- Cognitive assistance or emotional support due to confusion, dementia, behavioral symptoms, or mental/emotional disorders
- Grocery shopping assistance
- Telephone/communication device assistance
- Money management assistance
- Transportation to medical appointments

### Explicitly Excluded Services (OAR 411-034-0020(4))

The following are NOT covered under SPPC:

- Shopping (when separate from medical needs)
- Community transportation / mileage reimbursement
- Money management (when not specifically authorized)
- Social companionship
- Pet or animal care
- Yard work, gardening, or home repair
- Respite care
- Child care or babysitting
- Adult day services
- Home-delivered meals (separate Medicaid benefit)

Source: [OAR 411-034-0020 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_411-034-0020)

## 2. Covered Services — ODDS Track (OAR 411-455-0050)

Under the ODDS/Division 455 track for individuals with I/DD, IADLs are listed more explicitly:

**ADLs:**
- Personal hygiene (same as APD track)
- Toileting and bladder care
- Mobility and transfers
- Nutrition and meal preparation
- Medication management
- Delegated nursing tasks (medical necessity authorization required for individuals under age 21)

**IADLs:**
- Light housekeeping and laundry
- Grocery shopping
- Meal preparation
- Transportation to appointments
- Telephone assistance
- Money management

Source: [OAR 411-455-0050 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_411-455-0050)

## 3. Hour Limits — APD/AAA Track

### Standard Annual Maximum

**270 hours per calendar year** per individual served by APD or a AAA.

- Equals approximately 22.5 hours/month, or 5.2 hours/week
- The "PC20" name (Personal Care 20 hours/month) reflects the State Plan requirement: the Medicaid State Plan authorizes up to 20 hours/month (240 hours/year). Oregon's rule (270 hours/year) provides a small buffer above the strict 240/year calculation.

### Minimum Authorization Floor

**9.18 hours per service period** (to equal 20 hours/month as required in the State Plan) for consumers served by APD or a AAA.

**Service period = two consecutive workweeks (14 calendar days)**

So the minimum of 9.18 hours per 14-day service period converts to approximately 20 hours/month.

### How Hours Are Determined

Hours are determined by the CA/PS (Client Assessment and Planning System) assessment. The CA/PS tool produces authorized hours per service period based on the individual's functional need in each ADL and IADL category. Maximum task hours per ADL under OAR 411-030-0070:

| Task | Minimal Assist | Substantial Assist | Full Assist |
|---|---|---|---|
| Eating | 3 hrs | 8 hrs | 14 hrs |
| Bathing/Personal Hygiene | 5 hrs | 8 hrs | 12 hrs |
| Mobility | 5 hrs | 8 hrs | 12 hrs |
| Dressing/Grooming | 2 hrs | 5 hrs | 9 hrs |
| Elimination | 5 hrs | 9 hrs | 14 hrs |
| Cognition | 3 hrs | 7 hrs | 12 hrs |

IADL task maximums (OAR 411-030-0070): medication management (1-5 hrs), transportation (1-2 hrs), meal preparation (1-6 hrs), shopping (1-3 hrs), housekeeping/laundry (2-9 hrs).

Source: [OAR 411-030-0070 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_411-030-0070)

## 4. Exceptions to Hour Limits — APD/AAA Track (OAR 411-034-0091)

When assessed service needs will not be met within the 270 annual hours:

### Process

1. Local APD/AAA office completes **Form 514PC** (Exception Request)
2. Form submitted to **Central Office**
3. Central Office has **45 calendar days** to make a determination
4. Written notice required for all decisions (approval or denial)
5. Exceptions valid for up to **one calendar year**

### Supporting Documentation Required

- Signed Application Form
- Care provider logs detailing performance of all personal care tasks (as defined in OAR 411-034-0020(2)) for **one complete pay period**

### Denial Grounds

The Department may deny an exception if:
- The request is based on services outside of assessed service needs
- Assessed needs can be met within the 270-hour limit

### Renewals

If an individual has an existing approved exception, a new request must be submitted after the annual assessment is completed and reviewed prior to the exception end date.

Source: OAR 411-034-0091 (effective 12/23/2022)

## 5. Exceptions to Hour Limits — ODDS Track (OAR 411-455-0040)

### Process

- Individuals may request exceptions **orally or in writing**
- Case managers must assist with completing **Form 0514DD** (Funding Review and Exceptions Request)
- If needs assessment occurred less than 6 months prior: reassessment conducted upon request
- If 6+ months have passed: reassessment is **mandatory**
- ODDS has up to **30 calendar days** to approve or deny upon receiving complete documentation

### Exception Hour Maximums (ODDS Track)

| Assistance Type | Maximum Additional Hours Per Task Per Month |
|---|---|
| Hands-on assistance | Up to 2 additional hours |
| Ongoing supervision | Up to 5 additional hours |

### Documentation Required

- Description of how support needs are being met throughout the day
- Changes in assistance levels
- Available alternative resources and natural supports

### Denial Grounds

- Request not within exception limits
- Needs can be met by qualified providers within normal limits
- Assistance isn't an allowable ADL/IADL task
- Needs can be met through alternative resources or natural supports

Source: [OAR 411-455-0040 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_411-455-0040)

## 6. Assessment Tools

### APD/AAA Track — CA/PS (Client Assessment and Planning System)

- Conducted by an APD/AAA **case manager** (not a provider)
- Must be conducted in-person initially and at least every **12 months**
- Determines functional need across ADL and IADL categories
- Produces authorized hours per service period
- Document: APD Condensed CA/PS Assessment (updated July 24, 2025)
- Source: [Oregon DHS APD Assessment page](https://www.oregon.gov/odhs/providers-partners/seniors-disabilities/pages/assessment.aspx)

### ODDS Track — SPPC Needs Assessment (Form 2796)

- Determines eligibility and assistance levels for I/DD individuals
- Produces the **SPPC Service Authorization Plan**
- The Oregon Needs Assessment (ONA) is used for broader ODDS services and is distinct from the SPPC Form 2796
- DRO has documented concerns about the ONA's reliance on past usage rather than current needs
- Source: [DRO — The Oregon Needs Assessment](https://www.droregon.org/disability-rights-oregon-resources/ona)

## 7. Delivery Models

Participants choose between two delivery models:

### Model 1 — Agency Model

Care provided through a licensed **In-Home Care Agency (IHCA)**.

- Agency is the employer of the caregiver
- Agency is responsible for hiring, training, scheduling, and paying workers
- Agency must be licensed under OAR 333-536 (OHA licensing) AND meet provider enrollment requirements under OAR 411-033 and OAR 411-323-0065
- Contracted agency rate: **$39.40/hour** (effective January 1, 2026)
- Agency bill ODHS directly for authorized hours

### Model 2 — Consumer-Employed Provider (CEP) / Self-Directed Model

The participant hires, manages, schedules, and fires their own **Homecare Worker (HCW)**.

- Participant is the **employer of record**
- ODHS processes payroll through a fiscal intermediary
- The participant-employer relationship is governed by OAR 411-030-0040 and OAR 411-031
- HCW wage rate: **$21.25/hour** (effective January 1, 2026, per SEIU collective bargaining agreement)
- **Critical restriction:** A spouse CANNOT be hired as a paid caregiver under SPPC. (Spouses can be paid under the Spousal Pay Program OAR 411-030-0080 or Independent Choices Program OAR 411-030-0100.)

Sources:
- [OAR 411-030-0040 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_411-030-0040)
