# Instructions for Filing Helen's Complaint and Hearing Request

There are three things to do. They each serve a different purpose. Do all three.

---

## 1. File the Administrative Hearing Request

**This protects Helen's individual case.** It creates a legal record and forces ODHS to respond.

- **Form:** DHS 443 ("Request for Hearing")
- **Where to get it:** Ask the La Grande APD office for a copy, or call the ODHS main line at 1-800-282-8096 and ask them to mail or email you a DHS 443 form
- **Deadline:** You have 45 days from the date of the denial notice to request a hearing. Helen's assessment was issued February 11, 2026 -- do not wait
- **What to write on the form:** Keep it simple. You do not need to make the full legal argument on the form. Just state:
  - "I am requesting a hearing because Helen Mathson was incorrectly scored as Independent in Bathing"
  - "The caseworker wrote that stand-by assist is not a qualified need per OAR. This is incorrect. OAR 411-015-0006(2)(a)(A) explicitly includes stand-by presence as a qualifying assist"
  - "Helen should have been scored as Assist in Bathing and assigned SPL 17, not SPL 99"
- **Where to send it:** The address will be on the denial notice. If not, mail it to:
  - ODHS Office of Administrative Hearings
  - 500 Summer Street NE, E-48
  - Salem, OR 97301
- **Keep a copy of everything you send**

---

## 2. Submit the GAO Intake

**This gets your complaint into the Governor's Advocacy Office system.**

- **Email:** gao.info@odhs.oregon.gov
- **Phone:** 1-800-282-8096 (ask to be connected to the Governor's Advocacy Office)
- **What to say or write:** Tell them you are filing a complaint about a caseworker at the La Grande APD branch who incorrectly denied OPI-M benefits by misapplying OAR 411-015-0006. Give them Helen's name and Prime number (AI80366A). They will walk you through their intake process or send you their form.
- **This step is quick.** It just gets you into their system so there is a record.

---

## 3. Send the Letter

**This is the piece that asks for the audit and retraining. The form and the hearing request cannot do this.**

The letter is saved on the USB / in the folder as **MATHSON_GAO_COMPLAINT_FINAL.docx**.

Before sending:

1. Open the file in Word
2. Fill in your name, address, phone, and email at the top
3. Print it, sign it, and date it
4. Make 4 copies:
   - **Copy 1:** Mail to the Governor's Advocacy Office (address is on the letter)
   - **Copy 2:** Mail to the Branch 3111 manager in La Grande (address is on the letter)
   - **Copy 3:** Mail to APD Central Office Quality Assurance in Salem (address is on the letter)
   - **Copy 4:** Keep for your records
5. Also email a PDF or scan of the signed letter to gao.info@odhs.oregon.gov

**Send Copies 1 and 3 by certified mail with return receipt.** This costs about $4 each at the post office. The green card that comes back is your proof they received it. Copy 2 to the branch manager can go regular mail.

## If They Do Not Respond

If 14 days pass after they receive the letter and you have not gotten a written response:

- Call the GAO office and ask for a status update. Write down who you spoke to and what they said.
- If they have no answer, that silence becomes evidence. At that point, escalate to:
  - Oregon Long-Term Care Ombudsman: 1-800-522-2602
  - ODHS Director's Office: 503-945-5944
  - Your state representative and state senator (they can make direct inquiries to ODHS on your behalf)
