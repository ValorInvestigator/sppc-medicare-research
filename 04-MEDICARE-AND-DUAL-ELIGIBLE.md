# Medicare and SPPC — Dual-Eligible Coordination

> Research compiled February 28 – March 1, 2026.

---

## 1. The Core Rule: Medicare Does NOT Cover Personal Care

This is the foundational fact driving SPPC's existence for dual-eligible individuals.

Medicare **explicitly excludes** custodial and personal care assistance with activities of daily living (bathing, dressing, toileting, mobility, eating) when that is the only care needed. This is a categorical exclusion — not a coverage limitation or frequency restriction. Personal care as a standalone service is simply not a Medicare benefit.

Per Medicare.gov: "Medicaid offers benefits not normally covered by Medicare, like nursing home care and personal care services."

Sources:
- [Medicare Home Health Services Coverage — Medicare.gov](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/home-health-services)
- [Medicaid and Medicare — Medicare.gov](https://www.medicare.gov/basics/costs/help/medicaid)
- [Personal Care Services — CMS.gov](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/medicaid-coordination/states/personal-care-services)

## 2. What Medicare DOES Cover (Home Health — Not the Same as Personal Care)

Medicare covers **skilled home health services** under specific conditions, which are different from personal care:

- **Skilled nursing care** (wound care, medication management, injections, health monitoring) — provided by an RN or LPN
- **Physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech-language pathology**
- **Home health aide services** — but ONLY when provided alongside skilled nursing or therapy, and ONLY for medical care (not purely custodial ADL assistance)
- **Medical social services**
- **Durable medical equipment (DME)**

Medicare home health requires:
1. A physician's order/plan of care
2. The individual is **homebound** (leaving home requires considerable effort)
3. The need is for **skilled care** (not merely custodial/personal care)
4. Care is provided by a Medicare-certified home health agency

**Key distinction:** A Medicare home health aide can help with bathing/dressing ONLY as part of a skilled care plan. If the person needs help with bathing but has no skilled nursing need, Medicare won't pay for it. Medicaid SPPC fills that gap.

Source: [eCFR 42 CFR Part 484 — Home Health Services](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-42/chapter-IV/subchapter-G/part-484)

## 3. Dual-Eligible Payment Rules

For individuals who have both Medicare and full Medicaid (dual eligibles):

**Medicare pays first.** For any service covered by Medicare (hospital, doctor, skilled nursing), Medicare is the primary payer. Medicaid pays last — after Medicare and any other insurance.

Per Medicare.gov: "Medicare pays first when you're a dual eligible and you get Medicare-covered services. Medicaid pays last, after Medicare and any other health insurance you have."

For personal care services (SPPC): Medicare does not cover it at all, so Medicaid (SPPC) is the **sole payer**. There is no Medicare coordination for SPPC — it is entirely a Medicaid expense.

Additional dual-eligible benefits:
- State pays Medicare Part B monthly premiums
- State may pay Medicare cost-sharing (deductibles, coinsurance, copayments)
- Automatic Extra Help with Medicare Part D drug costs
- Medicaid may cover drugs Medicare doesn't cover

## 4. Dual-Eligible Special Needs Plans (D-SNPs) in Oregon

Oregon has several Medicare Advantage Dual Eligible Special Needs Plans (D-SNPs) that combine Medicare and Medicaid benefits. These are available to individuals who qualify for BOTH Medicare AND full Medicaid.

### CareOregon Advantage Plus (HMO D-SNP)

- Combines Original Medicare (Part A, B), Part D prescription drugs, and Medicaid benefits
- Premium: $0/month
- Deductible: $0
- Covers: eye exams/glasses, meal delivery after hospital stays, in-home wellness visits, **in-home support services**, 24/7 personal medical alert system, dental, hearing aids, medical transportation, $186/year CareCard for OTC items
- "HMO D-SNP with a Medicare/Medicaid contract"
- Source: [CareOregon Advantage Plus](https://www.careoregonadvantage.org/our-plan/explore-our-plan)

### PacificSource Dual Care (HMO D-SNP) — 2026

- Combines Original Medicare, Part D, and Medicaid benefits
- Eligibility: full Medicaid + Medicare required
- Premium: $0
- Covers standard Medicare services at $0 copay plus supplemental dental, vision, fitness (One Pass), OTC allowance ($130/quarter), NEMT
- Source: [PacificSource Dual Care 2026](https://medicare.pacificsource.com/Find/2026/DSNP/PacificSourceDualCare)

### Important: D-SNPs Do NOT Replace SPPC

D-SNP plans combine Medicare and some Medicaid benefits, but **LTSS including SPPC are carved out of managed care (including D-SNPs)** in Oregon. SPPC is administered by ODHS/APD fee-for-service. A person enrolled in a D-SNP still has their SPPC authorized and paid by ODHS/APD separately — the D-SNP does not control or fund SPPC hours.

The "in-home support services" listed as a D-SNP supplemental benefit is a limited supplemental add-on, not a replacement for the full SPPC entitlement.

## 5. Oregon's Medicaid Personal Care Status

Per KFF State Health Facts (2018 data, still current): Oregon covers personal care services through its Medicaid program with status **"Yes — CN"** (categorically needy populations covered).

Oregon's specification: "Personal care services are limited to 20 hours per month. Individuals whose assessed need exceeds the 20 hour limit may receive approval for additional hours through a prior approval process."

As of 2018, **34 states** offered personal care as a Medicaid state plan benefit; 17 did not.

Source: [KFF — Medicaid Benefits: Personal Care Services by State](https://www.kff.org/medicaid/state-indicator/personal-care-services/)

## 6. CMS National Policy on Personal Care

Two Medicaid authorities cover personal care services nationally:

1. **Section 1905(a)(24) state plan personal care** — what Oregon's SPPC is. An optional state plan benefit. No institutional level of care requirement. Entitlement for all eligible individuals. Can be self-directed.

2. **Section 1915(c) HCBS waivers** — waiver authority that can include personal care. Requires institutional level of care standard. Can have enrollment caps and waitlists. More flexible in what can be covered but less of a guaranteed entitlement.

Per MACPAC March 2025 Report to Congress: "Section 1905(a)(24) allows states to cover personal care services under the state plan but does not give beneficiaries the authority to manage a personal budget [without additional authority]."

Source: [MACPAC March 2025 Report — Streamlining Medicaid Section 1915 Authorities](https://www.macpac.gov/publication/streamlining-medicaid-section-1915-authorities-for-home-and-community-based-services/)

## 7. National Research: States Offering Personal Care (State Plan vs. Waiver)

From PMC/NCBI — State Medicaid Programs Offering Personal Care Services:

Personal care services under the Medicaid state plan are distinct from waiver-based personal care in several ways:
- State plan personal care does NOT require an institutional level of care determination
- State plan personal care is available to all eligible individuals (entitlement)
- States that offer it through waivers can limit enrollment
- Oregon offers SPPC through the state plan AND offers broader services through K Plan (1915k CFC option)

Source: [State Medicaid Programs Offering Personal Care Services — PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4194739/)

## 8. Practical Implications for Dual-Eligible Individuals on SPPC

**Scenario: Person age 68, Medicare + full Medicaid (OSIPM), needs help with bathing and dressing daily, no skilled nursing need.**

- Medicare: Does NOT cover personal care. Will not pay for bathing/dressing assistance.
- SPPC (Medicaid): Covers bathing/dressing. Person is assessed by APD case manager via CA/PS. If authorized hours are set at, say, 60 hrs/quarter, homecare worker provides daily bathing/dressing assistance.
- Medicare pays for doctor visits, hospital, Part D drugs.
- ODHS/APD pays the homecare worker or agency for the SPPC personal care hours.
- CCO coordinates medical care but does NOT authorize or pay SPPC.
- If the person also needs skilled wound care: Medicare home health covers the skilled nursing visits. The Medicare home health aide may also assist with bathing during those skilled visits. But the standalone daily personal care is still SPPC.

**The gap Medicare leaves** is exactly what SPPC fills. For Oregon's 100,000+ dual-eligible adults, SPPC is often the program keeping them out of nursing homes.
