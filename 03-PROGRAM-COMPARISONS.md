## 2. SPPC vs. K Plan — Key Differences

### The Level-of-Care Threshold

This is the central distinction. SPPC and K Plan serve non-overlapping populations by level of care:

- **SPPC:** Below nursing facility level of care. One ADL need occurring at least monthly is sufficient. No CAPS score required.
- **K Plan:** AT nursing facility level of care. Individual must score in service priority levels 1-13 on the CAPS (Client Assessment and Planning System), reflecting inability to perform multiple ADLs and IADLs without assistance.

These programs are **mutually exclusive** for any given individual at a given time. OAR 411-034-0030 bars SPPC enrollment if the individual is "already receiving comparable Medicaid HCBS" — which includes K Plan.

### What K Plan Is

Oregon's K Plan is the state's implementation of the federal **Community First Choice (CFC) option** under Section 1915(k) of the Social Security Act, enacted through the ACA. It is a state plan amendment, NOT a waiver. This means:

- It is an entitlement (no waitlist possible)
- It generates a **6% enhanced federal matching rate (FMAP)** that waivers do not
- CMS approved Oregon's K Plan as a State Plan Amendment effective January 6, 2015

When K Plan was implemented, personal care services that had been provided under Oregon's 1915(c) APD waiver were moved to K Plan to capture the enhanced FMAP. The APD 1915(c) waiver was restructured to cover only three non-personal-care services (see Section 4).

### Income and Asset Comparison

| | SPPC | K Plan |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly income limit (single) | $967 (100% FBR) | $2,901 (300% FBR) |
| Monthly income limit (couple) | $1,450 | $4,352 |
| Asset limit (single) | $2,000 | $2,000 |
| Community Spouse Resource Allowance | Not available | Up to $157,920 |
| Monthly Maintenance Needs Allowance | Not available | Available for non-applicant spouse |

### Services

K Plan covers everything SPPC does, plus:
- Home modifications (ramps, grab bars, door openers, stair lifts)
- Home-delivered meals
- Durable medical equipment beyond what Medicare covers
- Relief care and backup systems
- Community transition services (moving from nursing home to community)

### Settings

- **SPPC:** Only in the individual's own home or a family member's/friend's home. Residents of any licensed 24-hour residential program are ineligible.
- **K Plan:** Own home, friend/relative's home, adult foster home, assisted living facility, or memory care facility.

## 3. SPPC vs. APD 1915(c) Waiver — Current State

### What the APD Waiver Covers NOW

After the K Plan was implemented, the APD 1915(c) waiver (Waiver #0185, approved through 2026) was restructured. Personal care and attendant services moved to K Plan. The waiver now covers ONLY three services:

1. **Waiver case management** — for eligible individuals
2. **Community transition services** — one-time setup costs when moving from institutional to community settings (furniture, deposits, household goods)
3. **Housing support services** — helping people secure or maintain housing

### Why the Waiver Has a Waitlist

The APD 1915(c) waiver has approximately **44,648 annual enrollment slots**. When full, it operates a waitlist. Waivers can have waitlists because they operate under a federal waiver of the free-choice-of-provider and comparability requirements — the tradeoff is that enrollment can be capped.

SPPC and K Plan, as state plan benefits/amendments, CANNOT have waitlists. All eligible individuals are entitled to services immediately.

### Who the APD Waiver Targets Now

The current APD waiver specifically targets people residing in nursing homes who want to return to the community. It does NOT provide personal care services for people living at home.

## 4. SPPC vs. OPI-M (Oregon Project Independence Medicaid)

### What OPI-M Is

Oregon Project Independence Medicaid (OPI-M) is Oregon's Section 1115(a) Demonstration Waiver, approved by CMS February 13, 2024, operational June 1, 2024, full implementation March 1, 2025.

OPI-M targets people **NOT already enrolled in Medicaid LTSS** who need personal care assistance but have too much income or assets for SPPC/OSIPM.

### How OPI-M Differs from SPPC

| Feature | SPPC | OPI-M |
|---|---|---|
| Income limit | $967/mo (100% FBR) | ~$5,217/mo (400% FPL) |
| Asset limit | $2,000 | $99,656 |
| MERP estate recovery | Yes | NO |
| Nursing facility LOC required | No | No |
| Concurrent LTSS enrollment | N/A | NOT allowed (cannot receive OPI-M while on SPPC/K Plan/Waiver) |
| Hours | 270/year | Up to 40 hours per two weeks |
| Services | ADL/IADL personal care | Personal care + adult day + home mods ($5K) + assistive tech ($5K) + respite + caregiver education |
| Caregiver support | No | Yes — for caregivers providing 10+ hrs/week regardless of relationship |
| Continuous eligibility | Standard | 24-month continuous even if income/assets change after enrollment |

### Critical Note: OPI-M and SPPC Are Mutually Exclusive

A person currently receiving SPPC cannot simultaneously receive OPI-M. They must choose one or the other. OPI-M is specifically designed for people who don't yet qualify for Medicaid LTSS.

## 5. SPPC vs. Independent Choices Program (ICP)

The ICP is Oregon's consumer-directed personal assistance 1915(c) waiver. Key distinctions from SPPC:

- ICP **requires nursing facility level of care** (SPPC does not)
- ICP **allows participants to hire their spouse** as a paid caregiver (SPPC prohibits this)
- ICP has enrollment caps and a waitlist (SPPC has no waitlist)
- ICP provides a broader range of services than SPPC
- ICP uses a budget-based model rather than hour-based authorization

Sources: [Oregon ICP — Medicaid Planning Assistance](https://www.medicaidplanningassistance.org/oregon-independent-choices-program/)

## 6. Spousal Pay Program (OAR 411-030-0080)

Because SPPC prohibits hiring a spouse, Oregon provides a separate vehicle: the **Spousal Pay Program** (OAR 411-030-0080). This allows a spouse to be paid as a homecare worker under specific circumstances when the individual qualifies for HCBS services. This is a separate enrollment and payment track, not part of SPPC itself.

---

## 7. PACE

Oregon also offers PACE (Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly) for enrollees who are:
- Age 55+
- Require nursing facility level of care
- Live in a PACE service area

PACE integrates all medical and LTSS under a single provider organization and is mutually exclusive with all other Medicaid LTSS programs.

Source: [Oregon Medicaid LTC Programs — Medicaid Long Term Care](https://www.medicaidlongtermcare.org/eligibility/oregon/)

## 8. How a Person Moves Through the System

A typical progression as needs escalate:

1. **No Medicaid needed / private pay** — person manages independently or with family
2. **OPI-M** — needs help but has income >$967/mo; accesses personal care with no MERP, no NFLOC requirement
3. **SPPC** — income qualifies for OSIPM/ABD Medicaid (<$967/mo); needs ADL help but not at nursing facility level of care
4. **K Plan** — needs escalate to nursing facility level of care; automatically transitions from SPPC to K Plan; higher income allowed ($2,901/mo); broader services and settings
5. **APD 1915(c) Waiver** — if on waitlist for K Plan and needing community transition or housing support; covers those services while waiting
6. **Nursing facility** — if home/community care is no longer sufficient
7. **PACE** — if age 55+, in a PACE service area, and want integrated all-inclusive care instead of nursing home
