# Oregon State Plan Personal Care (SPPC) — Overview and Eligibility

> Research compiled February 28 – March 1, 2026 via dual-engine web research (Brave Search + Firecrawl).
> All OAR citations verified against Oregon Secretary of State Administrative Rules database.

---

## 1. What Is SPPC

The State Plan Personal Care (SPPC) program is Oregon's Medicaid State Plan entitlement for in-home personal care assistance. It is commonly referred to as **PC20** (Personal Care, 20 hours/month) in the Medicaid billing and provider enrollment system.

**The single most important structural fact about SPPC:** It is a federal Medicaid entitlement. Meeting the eligibility criteria guarantees immediate receipt of services. There is no enrollment cap and no waitlist — ever. This distinguishes SPPC from all of Oregon's Medicaid waiver programs, which can and do have waitlists.

---

## 2. Federal Legal Authority

SPPC is authorized under **Section 1905(a)(24) of the Social Security Act** as an optional Medicaid state plan benefit. States that elect to offer it must provide it to all eligible individuals on demand.

Federal implementing regulation: **42 CFR § 440.167 — Personal Care Services**

Full text of 42 CFR 440.167:

> "(a) Personal care services means services furnished to an individual who is not an inpatient or resident of a hospital, nursing facility, intermediate care facility for individuals with intellectual disabilities, or institution for mental disease that are:
> (1) Authorized for the individual by a physician in accordance with a plan of treatment or (at the option of the State) otherwise authorized;
> (2) Provided by a qualified individual who is not a member of the individual's family; and
> (3) Furnished in a home or other location approved by the State.
>
> (b) Family member means a legally responsible relative."

Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1302. Source: 43 FR 45224 (Sept. 29, 1978); amended 42 FR 47902 (Sept. 11, 1997).

**Key implication of 42 CFR 440.167:** The federal regulation does NOT impose a nursing facility level of care requirement for state plan personal care services. That requirement exists for 1915(c) waivers — not for the state plan benefit. Oregon's implementation reflects this correctly.

Sources:
- [42 CFR 440.167 — eCFR](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-42/chapter-IV/subchapter-C/part-440/subpart-A/section-440.167)

## 3. Administering Agencies

SPPC is administered by **Oregon Department of Human Services (ODHS)** — specifically two divisions with separate rule chapters:

| Division | Population Served | OAR Chapter |
|---|---|---|
| APD (Aging and People with Disabilities) | Elderly and physically disabled adults | OAR Chapter 411, Division 034 |
| AAA (Area Agency on Aging) | Local delivery agent for APD in many counties | OAR Chapter 411, Division 034 |
| ODDS (Office of Developmental Disabilities Services) | Individuals with intellectual/developmental disabilities | OAR Chapter 411, Division 455 |

**Oregon Health Authority (OHA)** determines Medicaid eligibility (OHP Plus enrollment) but does NOT administer SPPC services directly.

**CCOs (Coordinated Care Organizations)** have NO role in SPPC. LTSS including SPPC is explicitly carved out of CCO managed care budgets. (See Section 9 of this document.)

## 5. Program Purpose

From OAR 411-034-0000:

> "The rules in OAR chapter 411, division 34 are designed to guarantee that State Plan personal care services support and augment independence, empowerment, dignity, and human potential through the provision of flexible, efficient, and suitable services."

Services are designed to "supplement an individual's own personal abilities and resources" — they are not meant to replace all care, only to fill gaps.

ODDS track purpose (OAR 411-455-0000): SPPC services are "intended to supplement an individual's personal abilities and available alternative resources, including the individual's natural support system, for the individual to live independently at home and in the community."

## 6. Eligibility Criteria — APD/AAA Track (OAR 411-034-0030)

### 6A. Two Core Requirements (OAR 411-034-0030(1))

An individual must:

**(a)** Require assistance from a qualified provider with one or more of the personal care tasks described in OAR 411-034-0020; **AND**

**(b)** Be a current recipient of a Medicaid OHP Plus benefit package.

### 6B. The Medicaid Requirement — OHP Plus

The individual must be enrolled in **OHP Plus** (full ABD Medicaid) or the **Healthier Oregon** program. Standard OHP (MAGI-based expansion Medicaid) without ABD categorization does not qualify.

The relevant Medicaid eligibility category is **OSIPM** (Oregon Supplemental Income Program-Medical) — the primary Aged, Blind, Disabled Medicaid pathway in Oregon. OSIPM requires SSI-related categorical criteria (aged 65+, blind, or disabled under Social Security definitions).

**OSIPM/ABD Medicaid financial limits (2025/2026):**

| | Single | Married Couple |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly income limit | $967/month (100% FBR) | $1,450/month |
| Asset limit | $2,000 | $3,000 |
| Primary home | Exempt | Exempt |
| One vehicle | Exempt | Exempt |

No 60-month look-back period applies to SPPC (it is State Plan, not a waiver).

### 6C. Functional Eligibility — Lower Bar Than Nursing Facility

**SPPC does NOT require Nursing Facility Level of Care (NFLOC).** This is a critical distinction from the APD Waiver and K Plan.

The individual must need assistance "at least monthly" with one or more personal care tasks. One ADL need occurring at least monthly is sufficient. "Assistance" includes:
- Hands-on physical help
- Verbal reminders or cueing
- Redirection (for persons with cognitive impairment or dementia)

### 6D. Ineligibility Conditions (OAR 411-034-0030(2))

An individual is NOT eligible if ANY of the following apply:

**(a)** Receiving ADL assistance through a licensed 24-hour residential program (adult foster homes, assisted living, group homes, nursing facilities, residential care facilities).

**(b)** In a prison, hospital, sub-acute care facility, nursing facility, or other medical institution.

**(c)** Service needs met through the individual's natural support system.

**(d)** Assessed service needs are being met under other Medicaid-funded HCBS options (no duplication).

**(e)** The individual's **primary driver of need is based on an intellectual or developmental disability, a mental illness, or a substance use disorder.** (APD track only — individuals with I/DD access SPPC through ODDS/Division 455.)

### 6E. Additional Eligibility Provisions

**(3)** Payment is "not intended to replace the resources available from the individual's natural support system."

**(4)** Services are "not intended to replace routine care commonly needed by an infant or child typically provided by the parent."

**(5)** Services "may not be used to replace other non-Medicaid governmental services."

**(6)** Eligibility may be closed if the individual fails to employ a qualified provider for **30 continuous calendar days or longer.**

**(7)** SPPC must not duplicate other Medicaid services.

## 7. Eligibility Criteria — ODDS Track (OAR 411-455-0020)

For individuals with intellectual or developmental disabilities, SPPC is available through the ODDS track under OAR Chapter 411, Division 455. Key differences from the APD track:

- Assessment tool: **SPPC Needs Assessment (Form 2796)** instead of CA/PS
- Service period structure differs from APD
- Exception process uses **Form 0514DD** (not 514PC)
- Hour caps and exception thresholds differ
- No IDD/mental health exclusion (this track IS for IDD individuals)

Source: [OAR 411-455-0020 — Justia](https://regulations.justia.com/states/oregon/chapter-411/division-455/section-411-455-0020/)

## 8. Applying for SPPC (OAR 411-034-0035)

The application process for the APD/AAA track:

1. Individual (or representative) contacts their local APD office or AAA
2. Case manager conducts the **CA/PS (Client Assessment and Planning System)** assessment in person
3. Case manager determines functional need and authorized hours
4. **Service Authorization Plan** is issued specifying authorized hours and tasks
5. Individual selects delivery model (agency or consumer-employed provider)
6. Homecare worker hired; services begin

Contact: Oregon ADRC (Aging and Disability Resource Connection): **1-855-673-2372**
SPPC Coordinator email: **dmap.personalcare@state.or.us**

## 9. SPPC and CCOs — The Carve-Out

Per OHA's own documentation:

> "CCO budgets do not include long-term services and supports funded by Medicaid."

SPPC and all LTSS are paid **fee-for-service through ODHS** — completely outside the CCO capitated payment structure. A person receiving SPPC is typically also enrolled in a CCO for medical care, but their SPPC hours are billed separately to ODHS.

CCOs are required to coordinate with their local APD/AAA through annual **Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs)**. The MOU framework covers care coordination protocols, referral processes, and shared accountability for care transitions. CCOs do NOT control, authorize, or pay for SPPC services.

Source: [CCO and LTSS — OHA](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/OHP/CCO/Pages/LTSS.aspx)
