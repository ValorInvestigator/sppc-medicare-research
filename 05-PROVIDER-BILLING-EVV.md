# Oregon SPPC — Provider Requirements, Billing, and EVV

> Research compiled February 28 – March 1, 2026.
> Note: Some billing codes pending confirmation from additional research agents.

---

## 1. Provider Types

### Homecare Workers (HCWs) — Consumer-Employed Provider Model

A Homecare Worker (HCW) is a provider directly employed by an eligible individual to provide hourly in-home services. HCWs are NOT state or ODHS employees.

The relationship between a provider and a consumer-employer is that of **employee and employer** — the individual receiving services is the employer of record.

### In-Home Care Agencies (IHCAs)

Licensed agencies that employ caregivers and bill ODHS directly for authorized services. Must be licensed under OAR 333-536 (OHA) AND meet enrollment requirements under OAR 411-033 and OAR 411-323-0065.

### Personal Support Workers (PSWs) — ODDS Track

PSWs serve individuals with I/DD through the ODDS track. Similar requirements to HCWs but under the ODDS program structure.

## 2. Provider Qualifications — Homecare Workers (OAR 418-020-0020)

Homecare workers must:

1. Be at least **18 years old**
2. Submit a complete application and **provider enrollment agreement**
3. Pass a **DHS criminal background check** (rechecked every two years)
4. Be capable of performing required tasks safely
5. Attend a minimum **4-hour orientation** and pass competency evaluations (required before receiving a provider number — effective September 1, 2021)
6. Complete mandated **core training** within specified timelines
7. Complete mandated **continuing education** within specified timelines

**Orientation topics required:**
- Abuse and mandatory reporting
- Confidentiality
- Consumer rights
- Medication safety
- Preventing Medicaid fraud
- Providing person-centered services
- Universal precautions and infection control
- Worker requirements, roles, and responsibilities

Culturally appropriate orientations with interpreter services are required.

Source: [OAR 418-020-0020 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_418-020-0020)

## 3. In-Home Care Agency Requirements (OAR 411-033-0020 and OAR 333-536)

### Service Requirements (OAR 411-033-0020)

Agencies must:
- Be licensed under OAR 333-536 (OHA licensing) OR be a licensed home health agency with in-home care designation
- Complete initial screening before accepting clients
- Begin services **within five business days** from date of acceptance
- Develop task lists based on person-centered service plans
- Ensure staff carry identification
- Conduct criminal background checks through DHS
- NOT charge any Medicaid individual additional fees or penalties

> "Medicaid is the source of payment for the services provided" and agencies "may not charge any Medicaid individual additional fees or penalties."

Service scope: ADL support, IADL support, nursing services with medication management and administration.

Source: [OAR 411-033-0020 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_411-033-0020)

### Licensing Requirements (OAR 333-536-0010)

In-home care agency licensing via OHA:

- Written application on Division-provided form (demographic, ownership, admin info)
- Nonrefundable licensing fee (per ORS 443.315)
- Background check if owner/administrator has direct client contact (OAR 333-536-0093)
- Must update OHA within **30 days** of any changes
- Changes to administrator, classification, branch/subunit, or geographic service area require Division approval before implementation
- "No entity shall provide in-home care services or use the term 'in-home care agency'" without a current valid license

Referral services require licensure only if they schedule caregivers, assign work or compensation rates, define working conditions, negotiate services, or place a caregiver with a client.

Source: [OAR 333-536-0010 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_333-536-0010)

## 4. Provider Enrollment

### OHP Provider Enrollment

Providers must enroll with the OHA Medicaid Division Provider Enrollment Unit. Provider type for personal care is **Provider Type 30** (Personal Care / PC20).

Key enrollment forms:
- **OHP 3113** — Provider enrollment for Medicaid Division
- **OHP 3975** — Provider Enrollment Agreement

Separate applications are required per provider type. Providers must update OHA within 30 days of any change.

Contact: Oregon Medicaid Provider Services: **800-336-6016, option 6**
SPPC Coordinator: **dmap.personalcare@state.or.us**

Sources:
- [OHA OHP Provider Enrollment page](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/hsd/ohp/pages/provider-enroll.aspx)
- [Provider Enrollment form OHP 3113](https://sharedsystems.dhsoha.state.or.us/dhsforms/served/he3113.pdf)

## 5. Billing Rates

### Contracted In-Home Care Agency Rate

**$39.40 per hour** (effective January 1, 2026)

This is the rate ODHS pays to contracted in-home care agencies for SPPC personal care services. Rate set under OAR 411-027-0170.

### Homecare Worker (Consumer-Employed) Wage Rate

**$21.25 per hour** (effective January 1, 2026)

Set by the **SEIU collective bargaining agreement** between the State of Oregon and Service Employees International Union Local 503. HCWs employed directly by consumers receive this minimum wage rate.

Source: SEIU 503 CBA, OAR 411-027-0170 (rate schedule)

## 6. Electronic Visit Verification (EVV) — eXPRS System

### Federal Requirement

EVV is required for all Medicaid personal care services and home health services requiring an in-home visit. Required as of **January 1, 2021** (per federal 21st Century Cures Act).

### Oregon's EVV System: eXPRS

Oregon's designated EVV aggregator is **eXPRS** (Oregon's state platform for provider time entry and claims submission).

> "EVV equals eXPRS when used on a mobile device."

eXPRS is used for:
- Real-time clock-in/clock-out for personal care visits
- Service Delivery (SD) billing entry
- Time and mileage entry
- Claims submission to ODHS

### How Billing Works

For Personal Support Workers (PSWs) and HCWs:

1. PSW/HCW logs **Start Time and End Time** in real time via eXPRS mobile (or manual entry at eXPRS Desktop)
2. eXPRS records exact times to the minute (updated in 2015)
3. At payroll time: provider logs into **eXPRS Desktop** and reviews/submits draft SD (Service Delivery) billings
4. Agency "Claims Manager" role users can manually enter SD billing in eXPRS Desktop and select appropriate change reason before submitting
5. Claims submitted to ODHS MMIS (Medicaid Management Information System) for payment

### MMIS Billing Process

Oregon home care Medicaid billing involves:
- Manual MMIS uploads (for some agencies using billing software)
- Monthly EVV email submissions (for providers not using direct MMIS connection)
- eXPRS SD entry (primary method for HCWs and PSWs)

Contact for eXPRS issues: [eXPRS User Help Guides](https://apps.state.or.us/exprsDocs/)

## 7. Employer-Employee Relationship (OAR 411-034-0040)

Under the consumer-employed provider model, the individual receiving SPPC or their representative functions as the **employer**. The homecare worker is the **employee**. Key employer responsibilities:

- Screen and hire providers meeting qualifications
- Provide ongoing supervision, direction, and training
- Manage work hours, leave, and coverage arrangements
- Verify authorized hours are completed
- Address performance deficiencies and apply progressive discipline if needed
- Terminate unsatisfactory providers
- Abide by federal and state laws related to employer responsibilities

**Provider benefits:** Those negotiated in the collective bargaining agreement and as provided in statute. Retirement system participation is explicitly excluded.

**Representative option:** Individuals may designate representatives to fulfill employer responsibilities. The Department may deny requests based on: substantiated abuse history, child abuse findings, fraudulent billing participation, or previous failure to meet employer responsibilities.

**State as co-employer:** The state makes payments directly to providers upon verification of qualifications. The state functions as a co-employer for tax and labor compliance purposes.

Source: [OAR 411-034-0040 — Oregon Public Law](https://oregon.public.law/rules/oar_411-034-0040)

## 8. Household Hour Limits for Homecare Workers

When two or more eligible individuals live in the same household and both receive in-home services, the total authorized hours may not exceed **24 hours within any 24-hour period** in the same household, unless an exception is granted (OAR 411-030-0071).

Individual homecare worker constraints (OAR 411-030-0070):
- Single HCW cannot exceed **16 hours awake care** per 24-hour period
- New service plans limit workers to **40 hours weekly** (50 hours for those who exceeded that threshold in 2016)
